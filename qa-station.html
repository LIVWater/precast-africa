Manufacturing · Quality

Inside the QA station.

Vol. 08Five checkpoints every module passes before it leaves the plant.
I.  The reason

A defect detected on a building site is, at minimum, a snag-list entry — and at worst, a damaged handover and an annoyed family. A defect detected in the plant is, by comparison, a routine line in the production log. The economics of factory QA flow from that asymmetry: catching faults early is, by some margin, the cheapest thing a precast company can do.

II.  The reference

Every module has a drawing behind it.

QA on the floor only works if there is a clear, current reference to QA against. Every module type produced at the plant has a frozen engineering data sheet — dimensions, embeds, reinforcement, services, finishes, and a stacking detail — that the QA team checks against at each station.

The drawing is the contract. If a module does not match the drawing, the module does not despatch. There is no soft language at the QA station.

The factory is the only place in residential construction where a defect can be made routine, instead of regrettable.
— On Method · Precast Africa Studio
III.  The five gates

Each module goes through the same sequence.

Dimensional check first — width, height, length, diagonals, openings — against the engineering data sheet, with calibrated instruments and a tolerance band published in the QA register. Then a structural check — concrete cube tests at 7 and 28 days, embed positions and pull-out tests, reinforcement cover surveys.

Then services — plumbing pressure tests, electrical continuity and earth-bonding, ventilation flow. Then finishes — internal painting, joinery alignment, tile lines, sanitaryware sealing. Finally the despatch gate — module serial number, transport restraints, lift-point load test, and a final visual at the bay door.

IV.  Five checks

No module leaves without all five.

01 · Dimensional

Geometry, against the drawing.

Diagonals within ±3 mm. Opening positions, lift-point coordinates, and embed positions all calibrated and signed off.

02 · Structural

Strength & cover.

Cube tests at 7 and 28 days. Reinforcement cover surveyed with a covermeter. Embed pull-out tests at sampled locations.

03 · Services

Pressure & continuity.

Plumbing pressure-tested. Electrical continuity, polarity, earth bond. Ventilation flow within engineered band.

04 · Finishes

The handover, seen first.

Wall finishes, sanitaryware, joinery, tile lines. The QA station treats the module as if a family is moving in tomorrow.

05 · Despatch

Serial, tags, restraints.

Module serial number applied. Transport restraints fitted. Lift-point load test recorded. QA sign-off in the digital handover package.

Colophon

Quality is what the station enforces.

"Quality" as a word does almost no work. What does the work is the station: a known sequence, a calibrated set of instruments, a tolerance register, and a person whose signature commits the module to despatch. Everything else is description.

The QA package travels with the module to site, and the digital record stays with the developer at handover. The next contractor never has to take our word for it.
