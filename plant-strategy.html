Manufacturing · Strategy

Plant by plant, contracted to demand.

Vol. 05Why capacity follows offtake — never the other way around.
I.  The premise

The history of modular construction is, broadly, a history of overcapacity. Modular companies in the UK, Europe, and North America have repeatedly built factories ahead of demand on the conviction that the orders would arrive. When the orders did not arrive at the cadence expected, the fixed overhead crushed the unit economics — and the companies followed.

Precast Africa scales differently. Each plant is engineered against a contracted offtake, not against a forecast.

II.  Capacity as a contract

The unit of expansion is the plant.

A Precast Africa plant is a regional production unit, scaled to the development pipeline of one or more anchor developer partners. The plant is not commissioned until offtake is signed. The offtake is not signed until the modules are designed, the price agreed, and the milestone calendar locked.

The result is conservative on paper and resilient in practice: throughput is matched to demand by contract, not by hope.

A factory that builds the wrong building, on the wrong schedule, for the wrong customer is not a sustainability strategy.
— On Method · Precast Africa Studio
III.  The phased rollout

An anchor plant, then a region.

The first plant is the proving ground. It produces against a signed offtake. It trains the first cohort of permanent factory employees. It calibrates the casting, curing, and despatch protocols that will, in time, be exported to subsequent plants.

The second and third plants are regional units — each engineered around the development pipeline in its geography, and each delivered into a market where the system has already been seen working at scale.

IV.  Five principles

What we will, and will not, build for.

01 · Offtake first

No plant without a contract.

Capacity decisions are made against signed developer offtake — never against forecast demand.

02 · Throughput targeted

Designed to a unit-per-year band.

Plant footprint, casting beds, and crew sizing are scoped to a specific annual throughput, not a maximum theoretical capacity.

03 · Repeatable IP

The plant is the product.

Layout, equipment list, casting protocol, and QA workflow are codified — so plant two does not re-invent plant one.

04 · Local labour

Permanent jobs, sourced regionally.

Each plant is a local employer. Skills travel with the system; people travel with the work.

05 · Quiet expansion

No vanity capacity.

We will not commission floor space that is not contracted to a developer pipeline. The continent does not need empty factories.

Colophon

The company is built the way the buildings are.

If the buildings scale by modular addition, the company should too. A plant is a module of the company in exactly the same sense that a unit is a module of a building: defined, repeatable, and added when the demand says so.

It is the slower path. It is also the only one that survives a credit cycle.
