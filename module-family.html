System · Announcement

The PPVC module family.

Vol. 02 Three modules. One engineered variant. Every residential layout we build.
I.  The catalogue

A construction system can be measured by how small its catalogue is. The fewer components it needs to deliver any building in its range, the more reliably it can repeat — and repetition is the entire economic argument for industrialised construction.

The Precast Africa module family is, deliberately, very small. Three core modules and one engineered variant combine to deliver every residential typology in our catalogue — from a single-storey standalone home to a mid-rise apartment walk-up.

II.  On smallness

Why a small catalogue is a feature, not a constraint.

A volumetric construction system is only as fast as its slowest module. Every new variant adds a mould, a casting bed, an inspection protocol, a stocking line — and removes the volume that drives unit cost down. Singapore's PPVC residential programmes, which mandated volumetric construction for public housing above ten storeys from 2014, have repeatedly shown the same conclusion: the smaller and more disciplined the kit, the better the delivered economics.

We hold the family to four objects. Three core modules cover the structural and service heart of every home in our range. A single engineered variant absorbs anything that needs a different finish, façade, or specification — without breaking the system.

Repetition is not the absence of design. It is the discipline that makes the design buildable at scale.
— On Method · Precast Africa Studio
III.  The four objects

A short catalogue, repeated with intent.

A · Standard Living

The structural workhorse.

The module that repeats across every typology in the catalogue. Bedrooms, lounges, habitable rooms. Engineered for grid-stacking in reinforced 40 MPa concrete.

B · Wet-Core

Services & risers, finished.

Bathroom, kitchen, plumbing, drainage, electrical. The complex half of any home, centralised and pre-fitted in a single factory-controlled component.

C · Stair / Core

Circulation & structural spine.

Vertical circulation, stair access, and service integration for multi-storey blocks. The heaviest single component — and the one that lets walk-ups exist.

+ · Engineered Variant

The bespoke, contained.

A precision-built variant for higher-specification finishes, specialist façades, and unusual unit configurations — held inside the same modular grammar.

IV.  On engineering

What sits inside a single module.

Each module leaves the plant as a complete object: reinforced concrete shell cast at 40 MPa, internal finishes installed, services routed and pressure-tested, openings glazed where the unit specification calls for it. Up to ninety per cent of the building work for that module is done before it leaves the gate.

On site, only the joins remain to make. A thirty-tonne mobile crane lifts the module into position against pre-poured foundations. Structural connections — engineered for the specific module type — follow a published lift sequence. The site stops being where buildings get built. It becomes where they get assembled.

Colophon

The catalogue is the company.

Building a continent's housing pipeline means choosing what we will, and will not, do well. A small, disciplined catalogue is the answer to both. It is what makes the price hold, the quality reproduce, and the programme keep its dates.

The four objects above are the entire system. Everything else — every typology in our range, every developer partnership, every plant decision — follows from them.
