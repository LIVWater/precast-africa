System · Engineering

Module connections.

Vol. 09How volumetric concrete modules join — and why the connection is the system.
I.  The premise

A precast module is, by itself, a hollow concrete object. A precast building only exists once those modules are joined — structurally, against earthquake and wind, against thermal movement, against water, and against the failure modes that a developer's insurer cares about thirty years from now.

The connection is the system. Everything else is the unit.

II.  Lift first

The lift point is where the engineering starts.

Every module is engineered around the lift. Cast-in lifting anchors are sized for the module's full dry weight plus a dynamic factor (typically 1.4–1.6× under EN 13155 / ASME B30.20 guidance), positioned so the centre of gravity sits inside the slinging envelope, and load-tested before despatch.

A module that cannot be lifted cleanly cannot be installed at all — so lift design precedes everything: rebar layout, embed placement, opening positions, even the final external finish at the lift-point face.

If the lift is right, the building stands. If the lift is wrong, nothing else matters.
— On Method · Precast Africa Studio
III.  The four joins

What actually connects the building.

Structural join: cast-in steel plates and grouted dowel sleeves transfer vertical and shear load between modules. The detail follows fib bulletin guidance and is engineered for the worst-case load case at each module type.

Service join: pre-positioned plumbing, electrical, and ventilation connectors mate at standard couplings. Weather join: a continuous EPDM gasket plus a wet seal. And the architectural join: a finished cover that closes the inter-module shadow gap. Four distinct details, four distinct trades, all sequenced in the lift programme.

IV.  Five rules

What makes a connection work.

01 · Lift-led

The lift designs the module.

Centre of gravity, dynamic factor, sling angle — these set the module's anchor positions before architectural design is finalised.

02 · Standardised

One detail per join.

Every structural join across the catalogue uses the same detail family — different scales, same logic. The site does not improvise.

03 · Tolerance-aware

±3 mm at the module, ±10 mm at the plinth.

Site tolerances are wider than plant tolerances. The connection detail absorbs the gap without compromising structure.

04 · Weather-sealed

Dual line of defence.

Primary gasket plus secondary wet seal. Water that gets past one barrier still has another. No single-line weather details.

05 · Inspectable

You can check the join.

Every structural connection is inspectable post-installation — either visually or via a designed access panel. No buried surprises.

Colophon

The connection is the architecture.

Most of what an occupant ever notices about a modular home is the connection — the visible shadow gap between modules, the floor level transition, the seam at the ceiling. The engineering behind those millimetres of detail decides whether the home reads as built or as assembled.

We work on the connections the way we work on the modules: with a tight catalogue, a published tolerance, and a sequence that does not improvise on site.
